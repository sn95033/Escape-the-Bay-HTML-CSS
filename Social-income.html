Household Income Summary

As mentioned earlier, median incomes in San Francisco county are high (relative to other CA or out-of state locations) due to a large number of high tech companies in the city. The exceptions are San Mateo and Santa Clara counties in California, and Manhattan. Higher income is typical, both to afford the higher rents in San Francisco county and also to build equity to become a homeowner (most likely in the suburbs). The median income data is based on aggregation from 2013 - 2017, expressed in 2017 dollars.

The distribution of incomes comes from 2017 only and shows that San Francisco county has a much larger percentage of household incomes > $150,000, with the main exception being Manhattan.

With Manhattan data, the Median household income ($79,781), is not consistent with the 2017 Household Income distribution. A followup is to review the data to see if the distribution can be explained by significant inbound migration of more wealthy individuals in 2016-2017, or significant outflow of lower-income residents.
