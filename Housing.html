Housing Summary: Mortgages

Without a doubt, San Francisco County has some of the highest housing prices in the country, and thus, highest mortgaged amounts, with > 50% of housing requiring mortgages in excess of $1 million dollars (pink bar). Other high income counties, such San Mateo, Santa Clara, and Manhattan have a similar distribution of mortgages. Moving for the purposes of lower housing costs may not be an option in those locations.

San Francisco county has the fewest mortgages (~5%) valued at $500K or lower (red bar and lower).

Surprisingly, Contra Costa county and Los Angeles both have >50% of mortgages less than $500,000 (red bar and lower). Out of state King County in Washington state, Multnomah County in Oregon, Cook County in Illinois, and Travis County in Texas have a much larger spread of lower valued mortgages, suggesting that the economic benefits of lower housing costs could be a reason to move.

Housing Summary: Debt to Income Ratios

Debt includes secondary mortgages and other loans.

The debt-to-income ratios are high in San Francisco county. Surprisingly, all the top destinations in California, including Los Angeles have 50% or more housing debt to income ratios in excess of 4 (red bar). This suggests that overall costs are high in California, and only minor improvements to be found within these California destinations

Being able to move to a location with lower overall costs, as suggested by the debt-to-income ratios in Washington, Oregon, Illinois and Texas, may be a reason for the outflow of people from California.
